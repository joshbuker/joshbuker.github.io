What would life be like if you could recall any idea you've had before, in seconds, reliably? With consistent usage, and creating maps of content that match your brain rather than someone else's, fetching your notes on any topic is really that easy. This idea is called a "Second Brain" in productivity circles, the idea being that you save the day to day wisdom as it comes, rather than letting it fade to forgetfulness.

The tool that you use matters a lot less than the methodology and consistency, however I use Obsidian and have found it to fulfill my needs perfectly, so it's what I currently recommend. While Obsidian is safe from vendor lock-in due to how it stores data, if you prefer a fully open-source equivalent, you could also check out the Logseq project. For my recommendations in this blog post, you can most likely port them 1:1 to logseq.

## How to get started

I highly recommend a relatively flat and simple folder structure for your vault, and to resist the urge to organize your notes using folders beyond this:

- Dated Notes
- Evergreen Notes
- Attachments
- Bases
- Templates

Your notes themselves should fall into one of two categories:

- Dated Notes
	- Ephemeral notes that capture your immediate thoughts, and may be useful later, but that don't really need to be updated after you're done writing them.
- Evergreen Notes
	- Everything else
